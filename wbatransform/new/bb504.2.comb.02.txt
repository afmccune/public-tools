Blake, del. et Sculp. Publius Virgilius Maro. Poetarum Latinorum Princeps. Born 69 years before the Birth of Christ; was 7 years older than Augustus; lost his Estate, at 30; recovering it was nearly killed by Arius, the Centurion; some think he began his Eclogues, at 31; which took him 3 years. Commenced his Georgies, at 34, which occupied him 7 years; and at 41 began his Æneid, which employed him 11 years; at 52, Virgil died, 19 years before the Christian Æra. London, Published by Dr. Thornton, 1821.

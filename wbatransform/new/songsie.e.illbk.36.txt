36 Leopards, tygers play, Round her as she lay; While the lion old, Bow'd his mane of gold, And her bosom lick. And upon her neck, From his eyes of flame, Ruby tears there came; While the lioness. Loos'd her slender dress, And naked they convey'd To caves the sleeping maid. The Little Girl Found All the night in woe, Lyca's parents go: Over vallies deep. While the desarts weep. Tired and woe-begone. Hoarse with making moan: Arm in arm seven days. They trac'd the desart ways, Seven nights they sleep. Among shadows deep: And dream they see their child Starv'd in desart wild.

    A facsimile of Van Dykes pen and ink drawing of Jacob Sending Benjamin with his Brethren into Egypt , executed by William Ryland in 1762 for Charles Rogers 2 volume A Collection of Prints in Imitations of Drawings , 1778. Ryland used etching needles and roulettes to carefully reconstruct the appearance of pen and brush marks made spontaneously, imitating the look rather than the process of drawing. Yale Center for British Art.
